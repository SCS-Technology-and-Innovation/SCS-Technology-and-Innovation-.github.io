Hands-on option

Basic stage for the hands-on option

Modify the example code into one in which reinfections are possible. Think of (or look up) possible ways to model and implement partial or temporary immunity, either by vaccination or as natural immunity from having recently recovered. What would the parameters be for those immunity models?

Pick one immunity model, no matter how unrealistic, to implement and experiment with. Document clearly how you measure the impact of varying the parameter(s) of the immunity model.

Remember to clearly cite all sources and to share interesting readings and videos with your classmates.

In-depth stage for the hands-on option

In addition to immunity, one could also reduce the connections between the vertices of the graph or seek ways to lower the probability of contagion upon contact (such as distancing, wearing masks, handwashing).

Think of a way (or look one up) to try to compare, in some rudimentary fashion, which of the three options is able to dampen the epidemic peak the most (and/or push it further ahead in time to allow the medical system to prepare): vaccination, reduction of contacts, or lowering the probability of contagion?

Remember to clearly cite all sources and to share interesting readings and videos with your classmates.

Conceptual option

Basic stage for the conceptual option

Read about mathematical modelling in medical epidemiology and then look into the same aspect but for computer viruses. What differences can you identify, if any?

Remember to clearly cite all sources and to share interesting readings and videos with your classmates.

In-depth stage for the conceptual option

Look also into how other propagating phenomena such as wildfires, floods, or cascading failures in electric networks or airports are modelled and studied. Pay special attention to methods and concepts related to containment of the propagation.

Write a summary, optionally with (hand or computer drawn) original visualizations of at least three such phenomena, identifying potential containment methods for each. Cite your sources, but please refrain from reusing existing imagery as such. Instead, try to express your own understanding of the model (the elements, the means of propagation, and the containment mechanisms) in your own words and in a diagram of your choice and creation.
