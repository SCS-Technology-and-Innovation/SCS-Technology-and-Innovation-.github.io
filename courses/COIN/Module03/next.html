...at the SCS

Nearly any aspect of commercial, industrial, or public decision making involves tasks that would benefit from formal modelling to put together a description of "how things are now", a description of "how things should be", a set of "what could be done, in what settings, and with what effect". This is not easy to do in precise terms and may be hard to keep up-to-date, but if an organization can actually pull it off and find (or build) a suffiently efficient planner (an algorithm to find a short action sequence), the benefits in terms of efficiency, savings, or impact can be amazing.
